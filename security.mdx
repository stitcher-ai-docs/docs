This page sets out StitcherAI's trust boundaries: how environments are isolated, where the AI's access to data ends, whose credentials the AI acts under, and what baseline protections the platform applies.

## Tenancy & isolation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Organization">
    Your company — the top-level container, tied to your identity provider.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Environment — the isolation boundary">
    An isolated workspace within the organization (for example, one per business unit). Data and configuration in one environment are **inaccessible from another, even within the same organization.**
  </Step>
</Steps>

> 🖼 **Figure:** Organization → Environment → Pipeline — the environment is the isolation boundary. _(image: org-environment-pipeline)_

<Note>
  Isolation is the **default**, not a setting you turn on. Credentials for [data connections](/connections-and-credentials) are **environment-scoped** — they belong to the environment that uses them and don't span the organization. Identity and environment-scoped roles are covered in [Access & administration](/access-and-administration).
</Note>

## AI trust boundaries

By default, the AI capabilities your teams use reason over **only your own data**, within your environment. Some capabilities can draw on **anonymized, aggregated** signals across tenants — and those are **opt-in only, never on by default.** You choose the level:

| Level | What the AI can use |
| --- | --- |
| **Total opt-out** | No aggregate-scoped capabilities at all. |
| **Tenant-only** | Capabilities use only your own data. |
| **All capabilities** | Aggregate-scoped (anonymized, aggregated) capabilities are enabled. |

<Info>
  "Aggregate-scoped" means anonymized and aggregated — and it is always a deliberate choice you make. Opting out is available at any time.
</Info>

## Your AI platforms, your control

When the [Reasoning Engine](/reasoning-engine)'s agents reach the AI tools and systems your teams already use, they run **under your credentials and your policies.**

<Warning>
  StitcherAI **never bypasses your authentication, authorization, or audit.** Because an agent acts with _your_ identity, every access it makes is subject to the same permissions and recorded in the same audit trail as any other access in your organization — nothing is special-cased for StitcherAI.
</Warning>

The practical consequence: the access controls you already run — environment-scoped roles, and the GitHub repository access that governs your configuration (see [Access & administration](/access-and-administration)) — keep applying to anything the AI does on your behalf.
