AI spend flows through the same pipeline as any other cost — connected, normalized to [FOCUS](/focus), attributed, allocated, and built into [artifacts](/build/generate-artifacts) like a cloud bill or a SaaS invoice. The **model**, **workspace**, and **API key** already arrive in the data. What's distinctive is reaching _what the spend was for_. StitcherAI closes that gap in two steps.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enrich from provider APIs">
    Add deeper detail to what the data already carries: **break out input, output, and cached tokens**, and surface the **rates** and the **cost per component**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add definition with telemetry">
    Join token- and trace-level telemetry from your own applications to resolve spend to **feature, team, and customer** — the basis of AI unit economics.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 1 — enrich from provider APIs

The **model**, **workspace**, and **API key** are already in the provider's cost-and-usage data. Enrichment adds the deeper detail underneath those figures:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Token breakout" icon="layer-group">
    Separate **input, output, and cached tokens** rather than one combined count.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Rates" icon="tags">
    The per-component rate applied to each kind of token.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Cost per component" icon="file-invoice-dollar">
    The cost of each component, broken out from the single billed figure.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Fidelity preserved" icon="scale-balanced">
    The detail still reconciles to what the provider billed.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  This is enrichment **to the extent the provider's data supports it** — the same guaranteed-versus-best-effort principle as [FOCUS normalization](/focus). Provider data only carries so much definition.
</Note>

## Step 2 — add definition with telemetry

The definition that cost-and-usage data can't supply — _which feature, team, or customer drove the spend_ — comes from **telemetry**: token- and trace-level usage emitted by your own AI applications. Joining telemetry to the enriched cost data is what makes AI unit economics real.

With telemetry joined in, you can build artifacts that express AI as a business measure:

| Measure | Build it as |
| --- | --- |
| Cost per inference | A KPI in [Generate](/build/generate-artifacts) |
| Cost per feature | A cost-per-feature KPI |
| Cost per tenant | A derived dataset |
| Margin by feature | A derived dataset joining cost to revenue |

<Tip>
  Provider data carries the **model, workspace, and API key**, and enrichment adds the **token, rate, and per-component detail** beneath them. Telemetry is how you reach **feature, team, and customer** — and true unit economics and margin.
</Tip>

Once these measures exist, you can govern them like any other KPI — for example, alerting when cost per tenant or margin by feature crosses a threshold. See [Govern](/build/govern).

## What the data can and can't carry

<Warning>
  StitcherAI populates what the data supports and is explicit about what it can't manufacture. Provider data carries definition only so far; telemetry carries it further; definition that isn't present in the source is not invented. This is the same discipline applied to [FOCUS columns](/focus) elsewhere in the model.
</Warning>
