The Reasoning Engine is the always-present layer of IT-Finance agents that acts on your cost model inside your workflows. It's **agentic, not a chatbot**: a network of agents that plan and take multi-step actions against your model — with a human in the loop where it matters — rather than returning a single answer.

<Info>
  The agents reason over **exactly what the Semantic Engine produced** — the same complete, normalized, attributed model (see [the two engines](/architecture-two-engines)). Their plans and actions are grounded in your real numbers, not an approximation.
</Info>

## The agents

<Note>
  **Each agent is paired with a critic that reviews its work.** The critic checks the agent's reasoning and output before it reaches you, so what you see has already been challenged.
</Note>

You set StitcherAI as the **IT-Finance expert** in your AI platform, so spend-impacting decisions are reasoned against _your_ model — and the agents act **under your own credentials**. An engineer or analyst doesn't leave their workflow to get grounded cost, feature, and margin context.

<Warning>
  The agents run under your credentials and policies; StitcherAI does not bypass your authentication, authorization, or audit. The full trust-boundary model is covered in [Security](/security).
</Warning>

## Under the hood: MCP

The Reasoning Engine exposes itself as an **MCP (Model Context Protocol) server** — the open standard for connecting AI applications to external tools and data. Any MCP-compatible client can attach to it directly, which is what makes "reachable inside your own AI platforms" a protocol-level fact rather than a set of one-off integrations built and maintained per tool.

Once connected, an AI platform gets a typed catalog of capabilities to call: querying the cost model directly (with guardrails such as dry-run confirmation before a large scan), running what-if simulations, managing data connections, and orchestrating the coordination, planning, and custom-cost agents described above. Each capability declares its own cost profile, so a lightweight lookup and a heavyweight simulation are governed — and rate-limited — differently rather than treated the same.

<Info>
  Because MCP is the transport, adding StitcherAI to a new AI platform is a matter of pointing an MCP-compatible client at the server — not building a bespoke integration for every tool your teams use.
</Info>

## Orchestrating across your systems

The agents orchestrate multi-step workflows that span your systems — ERPs, ITSM, procurement, observability, and BI. The work is a sequence of actions across those tools, gated by you where it matters, not a single lookup.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Reconcile invoices">
    Pull, match, and reconcile invoices against the cost model — surfacing discrepancies rather than asking you to chase them by hand.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Run a what-if before committing capacity">
    Simulate a change and review it — with the agent's plan and its critic's review in view — _before_ you commit the capacity, so the decision is made before the commitment is.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Generate and submit a chargeback">
    Build a chargeback [artifact](/build/generate-artifacts) and submit it onward to an ERP — generation and delivery driven end to end.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
