[Build](/general/glossary) is the stage that turns modeled data into outputs. It has three parts: Generate produces [artifacts](/general/glossary), Govern applies [controls](/general/glossary), and Events records the changes that inform forecasting.

In the authoring environment, open **Stages → Build**.

## Generate

Generate produces artifacts from the modeled data. Each artifact is its own file; open **Generate** and select **Add item** to add one, or open an existing artifact to edit it.

You start by choosing an [artifact type](/general/glossary), such as a derived dataset or a time series indicator, then configure it through a guided form (source data, time settings, how the value is calculated, and so on).

## Govern

Govern applies controls to your artifacts. You define control groups, each pointing at a source artifact and holding one or more controls.

A control has a type, a failure-handling mode, and evaluation conditions that decide when it passes or fails.

## Events

Events records known or hypothetical future changes, plus past anomalies, so that forecasts reflect them. They come in two banks:

- **Predictions**: future changes to factor into a forecast, whether known (such as a planned migration that will raise compute cost for a period) or a what-if scenario you want to model.
- **Anomalies**: past anomalies to mark so they don't distort a future forecast (such as a one-off cost spike over a known date range).

As elsewhere in the authoring environment, select the **info (`i`) icon** on any field for its explanation.
