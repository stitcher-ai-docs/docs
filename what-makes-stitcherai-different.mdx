StitcherAI is built on five ideas, foundation first.

## 1. A comprehensive, transparent cost model

The foundation is one model that spans every provider and spend type, normalized to a common schema, in which **every row is attributable** and **every allocation is traceable to a rule you can read as a sentence.**

It's built from three inputs:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Cost data" icon="file-invoice-dollar">
    Cloud, SaaS, AI, invoices, and internal IT.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Business data" icon="sitemap">
    Ownership, teams, headcount, revenue, budgets.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Telemetry" icon="signal">
    Token- and trace-level usage from your applications.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Together these make the model _business-aligned_ — and make AI unit economics such as cost per inference, per feature, and per customer possible. Cost data alone cannot.

<Note>
  Because the methodology is transparent and auditable, the numbers hold up to scrutiny — and accountability for cost can move to the engineering and product teams who shape it, instead of sitting with a central team. **Visibility is table stakes; trust is the precondition for everything after it.**
</Note>

## 2. It plugs into your human and agentic workflows

The model isn't a destination you visit. Its intelligence is delivered where decisions happen — coding and AI platforms (Claude, Cursor, Codex, OpenAI), Slack, Jira, BI tools, ERPs. The [Reasoning Engine](/architecture-two-engines) places IT-Finance agents into those workflows so people and AI act on live financial context. Because you own the data, the same foundation also powers your own AI and automation.

## 3. Continuous governance

Governance is evaluated on **every run**, not in a quarterly review. Controls test the model against policy, and a breach becomes an alert routed to the owner _before_ the decision is irreversible — governance before the bill-shock.

## 4. Open by default

[FOCUS](https://focus.finops.org) — the FinOps Open Cost and Usage Specification — is the common cost language, and outputs are **open formats (Parquet, CSV) that you own**: portable and warehouse-native, not a proprietary API. A Kubernetes export, a SaaS invoice, and a cloud bill all speak one language.

<Info>
  You own and control the data — it lands in your own infrastructure and storage. StitcherAI replaces the IT-Finance _data-infrastructure layer_, not just the report. Where data is pushed from your side, it's written to your own storage with your own credentials, which never leave your environment.
</Info>

## 5. Finance as Code — made approachable

The cost model is configuration kept in a version-controlled repository: branchable, peer-reviewed through pull requests, and tagged by period so a past month reproduces exactly. Changing a large cost model feels like shipping software.

It's also approachable, not engineer-only — the same model can be authored through **visual forms**, by **AI chat**, or directly, with autocomplete, a schema browser, and AI always available to explain or build. See [Authoring & version control](/access-and-administration) for how this works in practice.
